Gmail — App Password Setup

Why can't I just use my regular Gmail password?
Google blocks "less secure" logins. You need to create a special App Password that's just for this newsletter app. Your regular password stays safe.
Already have 2-Step Verification on? Great, skip to step 2. If you just enabled it, you may need to wait a few minutes before App Passwords become available.
That's it for Gmail! You now have an App Password. Click "Next" below to enter your credentials.

SMTP Port587
UsernameYour full Gmail address
PasswordThe 16-char App Password
Daily limit500 emails/day (free)

ProtonMail — Bridge Setup

ProtonMail requires a desktop app called "ProtonMail Bridge".
Bridge runs on your computer and acts as a local SMTP server. The newsletter app connects to Bridge, and Bridge handles encryption. You must have Bridge running whenever you want to send emails.
Ready! Click "Next" to enter the email and password shown in Bridge.
SMTP Host127.0.0.1
SMTP Port1025 (check Bridge)
UsernameYour ProtonMail address
PasswordBridge-generated password
RequirementPaid ProtonMail plan + Bridge running

Tuta (Tutanota) — Important Limitations

Tuta does NOT support standard SMTP.
Unlike most email providers, Tuta uses its own proprietary encryption system and does not offer SMTP access — not even with a paid plan. This means you cannot directly use Tuta as the sending server for this app.

Your Options

Our recommendation: Go with Option A. Click the Gmail or Brevo tab above and follow that guide instead. You can still list your Tuta address in your newsletter as a reply-to contact if you want.
SMTP SupportNot available
WorkaroundUse Gmail, Zoho, or Brevo instead
Why?Tuta uses proprietary encryption, no SMTP

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) — SMTP Setup

Why Brevo? It's the best free option for higher volume — 300 emails/day on the free plan, and your emails are less likely to land in spam since Brevo manages sender reputation. Great if you don't want to use a personal email account.
All set! Click "Next". Use your Brevo login email as the username and the SMTP key as the password.
SMTP Hostsmtp-relay.brevo.com
SMTP Port587
UsernameYour Brevo login email
PasswordSMTP key (not login password)
Daily limit300 emails/day (free)

Mailgun — SMTP Setup

Mailgun is developer-oriented and offers 100 emails/day free. On the sandbox domain, you can only send to verified recipient addresses. Add your own domain for unrestricted sending.

Enter Your Credentials

Enter the email and password (or App Password) from the previous step. These are stored locally in your .env file — never sent anywhere else.

For most providers, this is your email address. For Brevo/Mailgun, it may be different — use what they gave you.

Use the App Password you generated, NOT your regular login password (unless the guide said otherwise).

This is the name recipients see in their inbox (e.g. "Jane Doe" or "Jane's Newsletter").

Back
Where is this stored?
Your credentials are saved to the .env file in the app directory. This file is in .gitignore so it won't be pushed to GitHub. Your password never leaves your machine (except when connecting to your mail server to send).
